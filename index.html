History of the Olympics

The Olympic Games began in ancient Greece around 776 BC as a celebration honoring Zeus. Athletes competed in events such as running, wrestling, and chariot racing. The modern Olympics were revived in 1896 in Athens by Pierre de Coubertin to promote unity and peace through sport. Today, the Olympics unite athletes from around the world in a global celebration of competition and culture.
